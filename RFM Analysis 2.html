二、Analysis of customer value and customer profitability (based on improved RFM model)¶

A study by Arthur Hughes of the American Database Marketing Institute found that three important indicators were found in customer data analysis, namely: the most recent consumption (Recency) and the frequency of consumption (Frequency). , consumption amount (Monetary quota), they are important criteria to measure customer value. RFM analysis is an exploratory analysis method.

  1. Classification criteria

    • Important value customers (high, high, high): recent consumption time, high consumption frequency and high consumption amount, absolutely high-quality customers.
    • Important customer retention (low, high, high): Recently, the consumption time has been long, but the consumption frequency and amount are high, indicating that this is a loyal customer who has not been here for a while, and needs to take the initiative to keep in touch with him.
    • Important development customers (high, low, high): The recent consumption time is relatively recent, the consumption amount is high, but the frequency is not high, the loyalty is not high, and the potential users must focus on development.
    • Important customer retention (low, low, high): The recent consumption time is far away, the consumption frequency is not high, but the consumption amount is high, which may be users who are about to lose or have been lost, and should be based on retention measures

  2. The role of the model

    • Identify high-quality customers, provide them with personalized communication and marketing services, and provide strong support for marketing decisions.
    • Ability to measure customer value and customer profitability.

  3. Disadvantages of the traditional model

    • The Frequency used is the number of times the user places an order at all times, and there is an error in the cumulative effect of time

  4. Improvements to the model

    • Use the total number of orders placed / the number of years of user-generated transactions as the new Frequency

# Create the function to classify different customer types
def customer_type_func(frame): 
    customer_type_list = []
    for i in range(len(frame)):
        if frame.iloc[i,2]<=r_med and frame.iloc[i,3]>=f_med and frame.iloc[i,0]>=m_med:
            customer_type_list.append('Champions')
        elif  frame.iloc[i,2]<=r_med and frame.iloc[i,3]<f_med and frame.iloc[i,0]>=m_med:
            customer_type_list.append('Big Spenders')
        elif  frame.iloc[i,2]>r_med and frame.iloc[i,3]>=f_med and frame.iloc[i,0]>=m_med:
            customer_type_list.append('Customers Needing Attention')
        elif  frame.iloc[i,2]>r_med and frame.iloc[i,3]<f_med and frame.iloc[i,0]>=m_med:
            customer_type_list.append('Can not lose them')
        else:
            customer_type_list.append('Ordinary Customer')
    frame['CustomerType'] = customer_type_list

三、Cohort Analysis¶

Cohort Analysis (Cohort Analysis, also known as group analysis), its main purpose is to analyze the changes in the behavior of similar groups over time (such as the repurchase of products, the use of products/services, etc.)

  1. Mainly include two forms
  • Initial behavior: such as "first useage of the app", "successful registration" or "first purchase behavior", etc.;
  • Retention behavior: It can be any behavior of the user, such as purchasing, using App, sharing, etc.;
  1. Analysis process
  • By grouping users according to the occurrence time of the initial behavior (to get the cohort), and then counting the occurrence frequency of the retained behavior in different time periods after the initial behavior time.
  1. Function and application
  • Cohort analysis allows us to observe the behavior of customers at different stages of the life cycle. Applicable to the data indicators we care about, such as revenue, user retention/churn, sharing and communication.

    if i>0 and i<7:
